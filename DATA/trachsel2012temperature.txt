ABSTRACT: 
We present a multi-archive, multi-proxy summer temperature reconstruction 
for the European Alps covering the period AD 1053-1996 using tree-ring 
and lake sediment data. The new reconstruction is based on nine different 
calibration approaches and errors were estimated conservatively. Summer
temperatures of the last millennium are characterised by two warm 
(AD 1053-1171 and 1823-1996) and two cold phases (AD 1172-1379 
and 1573-1822). Highest pre-industrial summer temperatures of the 12th 
century were 0.3°C warmer than the 20th century mean but 0.35°C colder 
than proxy derived temperatures at the end of the 20th century. 
The lowest temperatures at the end of the 16th century were ~1°C lower 
than the 20th century mean.

DESCRIPTION: 
Multiproxy summer temperature reconstructions for the European Alps 
covering AD 1053-1996, based on tree-ring and lake sediment data. 
Eight treering and lake sediment based proxies, from four different 
locations in the Greater Alpine Region, were utilized in the 
reconstructions. Nine different calibration approaches were used 
to reconstruct summer temperatures. The calibration period is 1760-1996, 
on JJA mean temperatures averaged over the region 43-49°N and 4-19°E,
using gridded temperature data. 

Greater Alpine Region: ~43-49°N, ~4-19°E
